# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

Contributors and maintainers of internal-admin are committed to keeping this
project a respectful, constructive environment for everyone — regardless of
experience level, background, or identity.

This is a technical infrastructure project. Participation here is a
professional activity. This code of conduct reflects that.

---

## Standards

**Expected behavior:**

- Communicate with clarity and respect.
- Accept technical criticism of your work without taking it personally.
- Offer specific, actionable feedback rather than general disapproval.
- Acknowledge when you are wrong or have been corrected.
- Keep discussions focused on the technical matter at hand.
- Be patient with contributors who are learning.

**Unacceptable behavior:**

- Insults, personal attacks, or dismissive language directed at a person.
- Harassment in any form, public or private.
- Discriminatory language or behavior based on age, nationality, gender,
  sexual orientation, disability, religion, or similar characteristics.
- Publishing someone's private information without their consent.
- Sustained disruption of discussions or reviews.
- Misrepresenting someone's technical position to make it easier to attack.

---

## Scope

This code of conduct applies within all project spaces: GitHub issues, pull
requests, code review comments, and any other communication directly related
to this project.

It does not govern what people do or say in unrelated contexts.

---

## Enforcement

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying these standards and for taking
appropriate action when they are violated.

Instances of unacceptable behavior may be reported by contacting the project
maintainers directly. All reports will be treated as confidential.

When a violation is confirmed, maintainers may take any action they consider
appropriate — including issuing a warning, removing a comment, closing a
thread, or removing a contributor from the project.

Maintainers who do not enforce this code of conduct in good faith may face
similar consequences as determined by other project members.

---

## Intent

The goal of this document is not to create a lengthy list of rules. It is to
establish a clear, shared expectation: contributions here are evaluated on
their technical merit, discussions are conducted professionally, and everyone
involved is treated with basic respect.
